# kame 𓂓

*Vital-essence-as-spiritual-double, received as cosmic-ordinance — the Egyptian ka fashioned at the wheel, carried as a decree of life given rather than made.*

kame is two true roots welded into one word: the Egyptian **ka** (𓂓 — *kꜣ*), the vital-life-essence the Egyptians held to be fashioned at birth by the god Khnum on his potter's wheel — animating the embodied person, surviving death, sustained afterward by the funerary "ka-foods" (*kau*) — and the Sumerian **me** (𒈨), the eldest word for a fundamental that was *received*, not made. So kame is the *ka* held as an ordinance: aliveness not as a spark a person authors but as a double handed over at the moment life begins, owed back a silent-reverent care. It is the cathedral's fourth life-side forge, the Egyptian (Afro-Asiatic, non-Semitic) angle on the one cosmic-life-as-creature-gift.

kame names vital-essence *specifically* — not personality, not name, not heart, but the aliveness underneath them that makes the body alive. Its light is the cleanest gratitude there is: being-alive held as received rather than self-made. Its shadow is the clutch — vitality mistaken for a possession one authored, or reverence curdled into a morbid hoarding that tends the dead and forgets to live. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **8.00/10**.
